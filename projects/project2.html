Avinocular

-- An autonomous mobile robot for aircraft skin inspection

Grade 10 | Intel ISEF

Summer 2019

Our ISEF project aims to create an autonomous mobile robot to reduce the workload of aircraft maintainers and perform automatic aircraft skin inspection.

For more information about my ISEF project, please visit this page.

My focus in this project

To program the robotic arm (We chose UR5) to carry out eddy-current testing to discover metal fatigue in aircraft skin.

My research process is composed of three main steps:

1. Collect data of aircraft surface

We use a depth camera to record the shape of curve and plan the movement of the robotic arm using 3D simulation.

2. Use 1:1 aircraft model to conduct experiment

Our goal is to follow the authentic shape of the aircraft surface as accurately as we can.

3. Try to inspect a real aircraft skin!!

We collaborate with an inspector in a local hangar and equip his eddy-current instrument on our programmed robotic arm to do real testing on an aircraft skin.

Additional component

We made a holder with spring to avoid damaging the probe of the eddy-current instrument. (Believe me, it is veryyyyyyy delicate:))
